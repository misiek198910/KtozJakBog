<h5>Day 5. The Greatness of Saint Michael the Archangel in the Steadfastness of Faith</h5>
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, called into existence in the same order as the other magnificent heavenly spirits, distinguished himself among them by his unwavering fidelity and steadfastness in faith. At the beginning of creation, God revealed to the angels the inscrutable mysteries of the Holy Trinity, the gift of grace, and the future Incarnation of the Son of God, demanding from them an act of obedience and the submission of their intellect. This trial was meant to prepare the pure spirits for the ultimate reward of eternally beholding the Divine Countenance in heaven.
<br>
While the proud Lucifer refused to humble his intellect before the creative wisdom and rejected the call to faith—desiring, as theology explains, to attain happiness and divine glory solely by his own strength—Michael the Archangel immediately acknowledged the absolute dependence of the creature upon God. He understood deeply that true and unchangeable joy flows only from the Divine source, and that salvation cannot be gained without the help of supernatural grace. The very essence of his name contains the fundamental confession: "Who is like God!". Standing in awe before the revealed truths, Saint Michael humbled himself before the Creator, proclaiming His unique holiness, goodness, and exclusivity in granting eternal happiness, thereby becoming the first defender of God's glory.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Prayer</h5>
<br>
O steadfast Defender of the Heavenly Kingdom, Saint Michael the Archangel! I stand before you as before the first and most zealous confessor of the Divine Majesty, confessing with contrition that in my daily life I have so often lacked a living and courageous faith. Aware of how close I have been to the ruinous pride that led to the fall of the rebellious angels, I beg for your powerful intercession and protection. Obtain for me from the merciful God the grace of a true conversion of heart, so that I may become a faithful and zealous disciple of Christ. Grant, glorious Prince, that my faith may be alive, unwavering, and bear fruit in deeds worthy of the Christian calling. Amen.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Greeting</h5>
<br>
Hail, Saint Michael the Archangel, powerful Intercessor who brings spiritual and bodily healing. Support me in every weakness and lead me by a straight path to God.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Task</h5>
<br>
Recite with deep reverence the Apostles' Creed ("I believe in God...") three times. In a free moment, go before the Most Blessed Sacrament to give thanks for the gift of faith and to pray for its increase for all Christians throughout the world.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Prayer to the Guardian Angel</h5>
<br>
Angel of God, my guardian dear, to whom God's love commits me here. Ever this day be at my side, to light and guard, to rule and guide. Amen.
