<h5>Day 28. The Greatness of Saint Michael the Archangel in Bringing Relief to the Souls in Purgatory</h5>
<br>
How full of mercy and bringing salvation is the intercession of Saint Michael the Archangel for the souls suffering in purgatory. A young boy named William experienced this vividly, whose state after death was revealed in a vision to a very pious monk. Saint Michael conveyed to him then that this deceased soul would not attain the joy of beholding the face of God unless someone offered fervent prayers for him to the Archangel—as the Prince and Guardian of human souls established by the Creator. The monk immediately undertook this spiritual obligation, encouraging many other faithful to join the prayerful assault as well. Thanks to this powerful intercession, William's soul was released from torments and triumphantly entered the heavenly kingdom.
<br>
Human souls that during life were characterized by a sincere devotion to this heavenly Leader also attain great consolation after the end of their earthly journey—for even in the place of purification, they have an unwavering certainty that they will not be left without his powerful help. How many profound reasons do we have, therefore, to love, praise, and surround Saint Michael the Archangel with constant reverence with all our heart!
<br>
<br>
<h5>Prayer</h5>
<br>
O most glorious Prince of Heaven, Saint Michael the Archangel! Deign in your immeasurable goodness to receive me under the wings of your powerful protection. I confess in humility that you are my best advocate, spiritual father, faithful comforter, and invaluable relief in hardships. With full confidence, I fly to your love—I beg, never abandon me, neither in the days of my temporal life nor at the critical hour of my death. Be my powerful defender and deliverer from the flames of purgatory. When I must bear the punishment for my sins, hasten to my rescue and introduce my soul into heaven, so that I may praise the Most Holy Trinity with you forever. Amen.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Greeting</h5>
<br>
I greet you, Saint Michael the Archangel, who with love and dignity lead purified souls into the eternal light of God.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Task</h5>
<br>
Request a Holy Mass for the intention of the souls in purgatory (especially those who during life venerated Saint Michael) or piously participate in the Eucharist and offer the received Holy Communion as a reparation for their sins.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Prayer to the Guardian Angel</h5>
<br>
Angel of God, my guardian dear, to whom God's love commits me here. Ever this day be at my side, to light and guard, to rule and guide. Amen.
<br>
<h5>Novena</h5>
<br>
Day Nine:
<br>
<br>
We beseech You, Lord, for the gift of Your great mercy. Deign to send to us Your faithful messenger, Archangel Michael, to carry our humble prayers before Your throne. May he himself instruct us on what gifts we are to beg You for, and may he intercede for us, ensuring Your fatherly care for the living and eternal rest in glory for the deceased. May the Lord of His grace send Saint Michael to constantly guard our souls and bodies.
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, powerful patron of our basilica, we unite with you in thanksgiving and praise of God for the gift of 550 years of presence and zealous ministry of the Pauline Order at Skałka in Kraków. We express our gratitude for our noble founder, Father Jan Długosz, and for all the benefactors who throughout the centuries supported the Fathers and Brothers in their holy mission. We humbly ask you that the fruits of this distinguished Jubilee Year may bring us all the grace of an authentic and zealous life according to the teachings of the Holy Gospel.
<br>
Through the powerful intercession of Saint Michael the Archangel and the heavenly choir of Angels, may the Lord grant in His loving-kindness that they may guard us in this temporal life, and after our death safely introduce us into the glory of heaven. Amen.

<h5>Pope Leo XIII Prayer to St. Michael the Archangel</h5>
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, defend us in battle, be our protection against the wickedness and snares of the devil; may God rebuke him, we humbly pray; and do thou, O Prince of the heavenly host, by the power of God, cast into hell Satan and all the evil spirits who prowl through the world seeking the ruin of souls. Amen.
