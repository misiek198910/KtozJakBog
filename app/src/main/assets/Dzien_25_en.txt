<h5>Day 25. The Greatness of Saint Michael the Archangel in Spreading Heavenly Peace</h5>
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel is called by the Church with deep reverence the Angel of Peace. This title fully reflects his mission, which consists in bestowing deep inner consolation upon human souls by forcing the powers of darkness into immediate retreat. Christian tradition uses three special designations to give him glory: Commander of the Hosts of the Lord, Angel of Victory, and Angel of Peace. It is precisely through his constant and victorious battle against demons that he obtains true, inviolable peace for the world and mankind. To whom else, if not to this courageous Archangel, should such a splendid name be attributed? He was the first to establish order in the heavens, ruthlessly expelling from them the rebellious angels who, by their pride and the war they initiated, dared to disturb the eternal peace of paradise.
<br>
I desire with all my heart to admire you, praise you, and bless you, O great Prince of Peace, Saint Michael!
<br>
<br>
<h5>Prayer</h5>
<br>
O most glorious Saint Michael the Archangel, herald of true Peace, Heavenly Victor, and powerful Prince of love! With the deepest humility, I turn to you today, asking for your powerful and effective intercession before the throne of the Most High. Obtain for me, dear Defender, the gift of lasting peace in my heart. Grant that inside my family and among my loved ones, inviolable harmony, love, and understanding may always reign. I trust boundlessly that through your powerful advocacy, the Holy Church will always walk the path of triumph, secure and full of Divine peace. Amen.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Greeting</h5>
<br>
Hail, Saint Michael the Archangel, heavenly Angel of Peace, who bring us certain victory in every spiritual battle. Pray constantly for our inner peace and for the eternal salvation of our souls.
<br>

<h5>Task</h5>
<br>
Undertake a small, personal mortification today for the intention of peace—refrain from complaining (mortification of the throat/tongue) or limit the use of the phone and screens (mortification of the eyes), offering this effort to God.

<h5>Prayer to the Guardian Angel</h5>
<br>
Angel of God, my guardian dear, to whom God's love commits me here. Ever this day be at my side, to light and guard, to rule and guide. Amen.

<h5>Novena</h5>
<br>
Day Six:
<br>
<br>
We beseech You, Lord, for the gift of Your great mercy. Deign to send to us Your faithful messenger, Archangel Michael, to carry our humble prayers before Your throne. May he himself instruct us on what gifts we are to beg You for, and may he intercede for us, ensuring Your fatherly care for the living and eternal rest in glory for the deceased. May the Lord of His grace send Saint Michael to constantly guard our souls and bodies.
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, powerful patron of our basilica, we unite with you in thanksgiving and praise of God for the gift of 550 years of presence and zealous ministry of the Pauline Order at Skałka in Kraków. We express our gratitude for our noble founder, Father Jan Długosz, and for all the benefactors who throughout the centuries supported the Fathers and Brothers in their holy mission. We humbly ask you that the fruits of this distinguished Jubilee Year may bring us all the grace of an authentic and zealous life according to the teachings of the Holy Gospel.
<br>
Through the powerful intercession of Saint Michael the Archangel and the heavenly choir of Virtues, may the Lord not allow us ever to succumb to deceitful temptations, but may He constantly deliver and protect us from all evil. Amen.

<h5>Pope Leo XIII Prayer to St. Michael the Archangel</h5>
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, defend us in battle, be our protection against the wickedness and snares of the devil; may God rebuke him, we humbly pray; and do thou, O Prince of the heavenly host, by the power of God, cast into hell Satan and all the evil spirits who prowl through the world seeking the ruin of souls. Amen.
