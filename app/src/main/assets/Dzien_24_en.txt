<h5>Day 24. The Greatness of Saint Michael the Archangel in Protection of the Vicars of Christ</h5>
<br>
With extraordinary zeal and power, Saint Michael the Archangel watches over the safety and mission of each of the historical bulwarks of the Church—the successive popes. It was he who broke the bonds and led the Prince of the Apostles, Saint Peter, out of Herod's Jerusalem dungeon. Later, in Rome, he shielded him numerous times from the deceits and magical tricks of Simon Magus. The Archangel also accompanied Pope Agapetus I, who in 536 undertook a difficult diplomatic mission to Constantinople to persuade Emperor Justinian to withdraw his troops from Italy. Before this dangerous journey, the Holy Father flew to the protection of Saint Michael, making a pilgrimage to his sanctuary on Mount Gargano. Thanks to heavenly intervention, the pope managed to bring Justinian I the Great into full unity with the Catholic faith, courageously opposed Empress Theodora who favored the schismatics, and ultimately saved Rome from ruin. Furthermore, the Archangel shielded Pope Gregory the Great during the bubonic plague that devastated the city in 590. He also supported Leo IX in his struggles with the Normans, who despite temporary military superiority humbled themselves before the authority of the pope and returned the seized fiefs. Miraculous defense was also gained by Pope Callixtus II, who precisely on the feast of Saint Michael, September 29th, received a solemn guarantee of freedom and peace for the Church from the emperor. This powerful intercession ensures that the foundation of faith of the successive popes remains unshakable and pure through the centuries.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Prayer</h5>
<br>
O powerful and glorious Prince, Saint Michael the Archangel, shield of the universal Church and special defender of the Successors of Saint Peter! To you, right after the Most Holy Trinity and the Immaculate Mother, we direct our trusting prayers in these times full of anxiety that cast a shadow upon the heart of the Holy Father. Deign to send your heavenly hosts to constantly enlighten, comfort, and strengthen the pope in this spiritual battle waged by the enemy of mankind. Pour profound peace into the heart of the Pastor and grant that justice may bloom and lasting harmony endure among nations. May the borders of faith constantly expand, and the fruits of good deeds multiply for the greater glory of God. Amen.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Greeting</h5>
<br>
Hail, Saint Michael the Archangel, zealous and tireless Worker in the vineyard of the Lord. Surround with your powerful protection the Bishop of Rome, the visible Head of the Holy Church.

<h5>Novena</h5>
<br>
Day Five:
<br>
<br>
We beseech You, Lord, for the gift of Your great mercy. Deign to send to us Your faithful messenger, Archangel Michael, to carry our humble prayers before Your throne. May he himself instruct us on what gifts we are to beg You for, and may he intercede for us, ensuring Your fatherly care for the living and eternal rest in glory for the deceased. May the Lord of His grace send Saint Michael to constantly guard our souls and bodies.
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, powerful patron of our basilica, we unite with you in thanksgiving and praise of God for the gift of 550 years of presence and zealous ministry of the Pauline Order at Skałka in Kraków. We express our gratitude for our noble founder, Father Jan Długosz, and for all the benefactors who throughout the centuries supported the Fathers and Brothers in their holy mission. We humbly ask you that the fruits of this distinguished Jubilee Year may bring us all the grace of an authentic and zealous life according to the teachings of the Holy Gospel.
<br>
Through the powerful intercession of Saint Michael the Archangel and the heavenly choir of Virtues, may the Lord effectively protect our souls from all snares, traps, and temptations of the devil. Amen.

<h5>Pope Leo XIII Prayer to St. Michael the Archangel</h5>
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, defend us in battle, be our protection against the wickedness and snares of the devil; may God rebuke him, we humbly pray; and do thou, O Prince of the heavenly host, by the power of God, cast into hell Satan and all the evil spirits who prowl through the world seeking the ruin of souls. Amen.
