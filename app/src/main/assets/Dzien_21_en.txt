<h5>Day 21. The Greatness of Saint Michael the Archangel in Love for Afflicted Faithful</h5>
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel surrounds all those who are downcast and wounded in spirit with special care, pouring into their hearts the refreshing balsam of heavenly consolation. When the mention of the Angel of the Lord sent by God (whom biblical scholars identify precisely with Saint Michael) appears for the first time on the pages of Holy Scripture, he arrives with a clear mission—to lift the spirits and comfort the suffering Hagar in the scorching desert.
<br>
It is worth noting that the messenger of heaven does not manifest himself in earlier dramatic moments of history, such as the great flood or the confusion of tongues at the construction of the Tower of Babel. All this happened so that humanity could clearly know the glory and goodness of this powerful Seraph, who tirelessly hastens to the rescue of the abandoned, comforts the afflicted, and brings joy where pain reigns. It was he who brought hope to Abraham and Sarah, foretelling the birth of a son in their advanced age. He also poured new strength into the exhausted heart of the prophet Elijah during persecutions and watched over the safety of David surrounded by fierce lions. Saint Michael remains the steadfast defender and advocate of everyone experiencing moments of anguish.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Prayer</h5>
<br>
Glorious Prince of Heaven, Saint Michael! I fly to you with deep confidence, acknowledging in you my wise father, faithful friend, source of comfort, and safe refuge. I know that through your powerful intercession, countless graces flow to me from God. If my life is protected from sudden misfortunes, destructive elements, and dangerous catastrophes, it happens thanks to your constant, protective presence, my heavenly Comforter! Implore for me, Saint Michael, the grace of your special assistance at the hour of my death. Comfort me in this final transition and lead my soul into eternity, so that I may endlessly rejoice in beholding the Divine Countenance in heaven. Amen.
<br>
<br>
<h5>Greeting</h5>
<br>
Hail, Saint Michael the Archangel, who bring holy joy and peace into the hearts of faithful Christians. Deign to comfort my soul in my present anxieties and sorrows.

<h5>Novena</h5>
<br>
Day Two:
<br>
<br>
We beseech You, Lord, for the gift of Your great mercy. Deign to send to us Your faithful messenger, Archangel Michael, to carry our humble prayers before Your throne. May he himself instruct us on what gifts we are to beg You for, and may he intercede for us, ensuring Your fatherly care for the living and eternal rest in glory for the deceased. May the Lord of His grace send Saint Michael to constantly guard our souls and bodies.
<br>
Saint Michael the Archangel, powerful patron of our basilica, we unite with you in thanksgiving and praise of God for the gift of 550 years of presence and zealous ministry of the Pauline Order at Skałka in Kraków. We express our gratitude for our noble founder, Father Jan Długosz, and for all the benefactors who throughout the centuries supported the Fathers and Brothers in their holy mission. We humbly ask you that the fruits of this distinguished Jubilee Year may bring us all the grace of an authentic and zealous life according to the teachings of the Holy Gospel.
<br>
Through the powerful intercession of Saint Michael the Archangel and the heavenly choir of Cherubim, may the Lord grant us the grace to leave behind all sin and faithfully follow the path of righteousness. Amen.
